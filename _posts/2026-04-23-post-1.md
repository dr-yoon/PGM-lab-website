On April 23, 2026, our lab attended the **71st Spring Conference of the Korean Society of Medical Genetics and Genomics (KSMGG)**,
held at the Baekbeom Kim Koo Memorial Hall in Seoul.
<div align="center">
  <img src="/images/2026_KSMG_program.png" width="700"/>
</div>
The program offered a rich overview of where medical genetics is heading
— from prime editing-based functional variant evaluation and brain somatic mutations in psychiatric disease,
to multi-omics approaches for metabolic complexity, multi-center WGS pipelines for ending the diagnostic odyssey in rare diseases,
and AI-driven liquid biopsy and precision immuno-oncology.
<div align="center">
  <img src="/images/KSMG_20260423.jpg" width="700"/>
</div>
We are especially proud that **Dr. Yoon** delivered a talk in 
Session II-B (*Clinical Translation of Genomic Data: Bench-to-Bedside in Rare Disease Research*),
titled **"From Sequence Extension to Disease: Integrative Functional Prediction of Stop-loss Variants,"**
building on our recently published work.
Many thanks to the KSMGG organizing committee and to all the colleagues who joined us for the lively discussions
— we came back energized and full of new ideas to carry into our ongoing work! 🚀
